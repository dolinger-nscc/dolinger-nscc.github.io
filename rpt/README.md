# Covid-19 Cases
This report is an ongoing analysis of the Covid-19 response primarily for Canada. Data for the United States will also be included for comparisons to identify effectiveness of distancing intervention.  
The report will leverage [Flourish Studio](https://flourish.studio/) for charts, graphs, and visualisations. 
 
## Accepted Information
The following is accepted as fact and may change as more information about the virus is established.
- Mortality rates, infection rates, etc. for the Covid-19 virus have not been universally established.
- Jurisdictions began isolation measures at varying periods after confirmation of infection in their jurisdictions.
- Isolation measures varied, implementation and enforcement also varied by each jurisdiction.

## Assumptions
This report makes the following assumptions and may change from time to time during report updates. Because of limited trace testing currently available the following assumption will be used in analysis and projections.
- Between 25 percent to 50 percent of people who are infected are asymptomatic. 
- The hospitalizations (if reported) and fatalities reported are the most "accurate" of all measures gathered again because of limited testing. 
- Jurisdictions vary widely based on density. For example, more natural isolation would be in place in rural communities vs large urban centres.
